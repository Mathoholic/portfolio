Think about it—would we care to know the heights and weights of students in a class if they all had the same height and weight?
What if all product consumers have the same opinion about the product—would we care to collect such an opinion? Similarly,
if the weather around you is the same all the time, would we care to note down the temperature every hour in such a case?
Quite obviously, this is not the case. Things around us are not the same in all respects; they are varying.
The reality is that our world is full of variability. We experience variability in our physical appearances,
and opinions about a product, and our surroundings are constantly changing. Such varying data related to different processes,
events, or subjects, when collected, needs to be handled properly so that we can make sense of that data.
The variability in data makes the existence of disciplines such as Statistics desirable.
